Sustainable Finance

Investment in Green Infrastructure

Posted on March 10, 2025

The G20 has committed to increasing investment in green infrastructure, such as renewable energy projects, sustainable transportation systems, and climate-resilient urban development. This is part of a broader strategy to meet international climate goals while fostering economic growth. By incentivizing private sector participation and public-private partnerships, the G20 aims to drive long-term sustainable development across the globe.

Climate-Resilient Development Funding

Posted on March 11, 2025

In a major push towards sustainability, the G20 has introduced new funding mechanisms to support developing countries in achieving climate-resilient development. This includes providing concessional loans and grants for projects focused on mitigating the impact of climate change, such as water management, agriculture, and disaster risk reduction.

Financial Instruments for Climate Action

Posted on March 12, 2025

The G20 has introduced new financial instruments to support climate action, including green bonds and climate-related investment funds. These instruments are designed to attract capital for projects that have a measurable positive impact on the environment, such as carbon reduction and sustainable farming initiatives. The G20 aims to scale up these financial instruments to drive large-scale environmental change.
